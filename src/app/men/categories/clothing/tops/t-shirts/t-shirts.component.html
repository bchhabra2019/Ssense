T-Shirts

An enduring casual classic, the men's t-shirt appears as a near-universal staple among contemporary designer collections. Notable for its incredible versatility, the garment has long proved a venue for experimentation and expression, with typography, logos, sloganism, photographic prints, and bonded graphics emerging as common tropes. Long sleeve styles often pay homage to the iconography and look of skate and streetwear culture, and practically speaking grant the t-shirt mileage into the colder seasons as a layering piece. Elsewhere, dropped shoulders work to create a draped and breezy silhouette. Cleanly tucked into trousers or jeans, the t-shirt assumes a simple utilitarian elegance, while oversized and untucked it presents leisurely swagger and lends itself easily to statement-making. Garment-pleated and technical fabric variants explore new possibilities for the form, diverting it into both more formal and more athleisure territories. Time-honored cotton variants take on distressing, fading, and other textural flourishes for character and comfort.
